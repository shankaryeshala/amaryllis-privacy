Privacy Policy — Amaryllis Boutique Admin

Amaryllis Boutique Dallas
Last updated: July 4, 2026
Effective date: July 4, 2026

1. Introduction

Amaryllis Boutique Dallas (“we,” “us,” or “our”) operates Amaryllis Boutique Admin (“the App”), a business administration application used by our staff to manage boutique operations, including customer communication through Instagram Direct messages.

This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, store, and protect information when authorized staff use the App or when our business interacts with customers through connected services such as Instagram.

By using the App or connecting an Instagram account, you agree to this Privacy Policy.

2. Who this policy applies to

This policy applies to:

  • Boutique staff and authorized users who log in to Amaryllis Boutique Admin
  • Customers who send Direct messages to our Instagram business account (we receive and process those messages through Meta’s APIs to provide customer support)

The App is intended for internal business use by Amaryllis Boutique Dallas and is not a consumer social network.

3. Information we collect

3.1 Account and authentication data (staff users)

When authorized staff log in to the App, we may collect:

  • Username or account identifier
  • Authentication credentials (stored in hashed or tokenized form; we do not store plain-text passwords in the client application)
  • Session and security tokens needed to keep you signed in
  • Device or browser information used for security (e.g., passkey/WebAuthn where enabled)

3.2 Instagram and Meta integration data

When our business connects an Instagram Professional account through Meta’s official login and APIs, we may collect and store:

  • Instagram Business account ID
  • Facebook Page ID linked to the Instagram account
  • OAuth access tokens and related credentials (stored securely on our servers, not in the mobile or web client)
  • Instagram Direct message threads and message content (text and metadata such as sender ID and timestamps)
  • Customer Instagram-scoped user IDs (as provided by Meta’s API)
  • Webhook event payloads related to new or updated messages

We access Instagram data only through Meta’s official Instagram Platform and Messenger Platform APIs, subject to Meta’s terms and permissions.

3.3 Customer and business data

In the course of operating the boutique, the App may also process:

  • Customer names, contact details, and order-related information stored in our business systems
  • Product catalog and order data necessary to respond to customer inquiries

This data is used to provide customer support and operate the business, not for unrelated marketing automation.

3.4 Technical and log data

We may collect API request logs, server and application logs for security and reliability, and IP addresses or browser type where applicable for web access. We do not use this information to track individuals for advertising purposes.

4. How we use information

We use collected information to:

  • Authenticate authorized staff and secure access to the App
  • Display Instagram Direct message conversations in a unified inbox for staff
  • Allow staff to read and reply to customer-initiated Instagram messages
  • Link customer inquiries to products, orders, or CRM records where appropriate
  • Receive real-time notifications of new messages via Meta webhooks
  • Maintain security, prevent fraud, and troubleshoot technical issues
  • Comply with applicable law and Meta Platform policies

We do not use Instagram messaging data to send unsolicited or bulk promotional messages, scrape Instagram outside Meta’s official APIs, or sell personal information to third parties.

Staff replies are limited to customer support and business communication within Meta’s permitted messaging windows and policies.

5. Legal bases (where applicable)

Depending on jurisdiction, we process personal data based on:

  • Contract / legitimate business interest — operating the boutique and responding to customer inquiries
  • Consent — where required for connecting third-party accounts (e.g., Meta OAuth)
  • Legal obligation — where we must retain or disclose data under law

6. How we share information

We do not sell your personal information.

Recipient Purpose
Meta Platforms, Inc. Instagram/Messenger API operations (send/receive messages, webhooks, token validation)
Infrastructure providers Hosting, databases, and secrets management for our backend (under contractual safeguards)
Legal authorities When required by law, court order, or to protect rights and safety

We do not share Instagram message content with unrelated third-party marketers or data brokers.

7. Data storage and security

  • OAuth tokens and integration secrets are stored on secure backend servers, not embedded in the mobile or web client.
  • Access to the App is restricted to authorized staff.
  • We use industry-standard measures such as HTTPS, access controls, and structured logging.
  • No method of transmission or storage is 100% secure; we work to protect data using reasonable administrative, technical, and organizational safeguards.

8. Data retention

We retain information only as long as needed for customer support, business operations, legal compliance, and resolving disputes.

When you disconnect Instagram from the App or request deletion of integration data, we delete or anonymize stored tokens and message copies according to our retention schedule, except where law requires longer retention. Message deletion events from Meta webhooks are honored where our systems support them.

9. Your choices and rights

Staff users may log out at any time and request review or correction of account information by contacting us.

Instagram connection: Business administrators can disconnect Instagram in the App under Settings → Integrations, or revoke the App’s access in Facebook/Instagram account settings (Settings → Security → Apps and websites).

Customers (Instagram users): You may delete messages in Instagram according to Instagram’s controls. Depending on your location, you may have rights to access, correct, or delete personal data we hold about you. Contact us using the details below.

10. Children’s privacy

The App is not directed to children under 13 (or 16 where applicable). We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe we have collected such information, contact us and we will delete it.

11. Third-party services

The App integrates with Meta (Facebook/Instagram) services. Your use of Instagram is also governed by:

  • Meta Privacy Policy
  • Instagram Terms of Use
  • Meta Platform Terms

We are not responsible for the privacy practices of Meta or other third-party sites linked from customer messages.

12. International users

Our business operates in the United States (Texas). If you access our services from outside the U.S., your information may be processed in the U.S. where our servers and service providers operate.

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. We will post the revised policy on this page and update the “Last updated” date. Continued use of the App after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy where permitted by law.
